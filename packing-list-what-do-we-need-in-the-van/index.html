Packing list: What do we need in the van?

Judith February 16, 2020

Storage on 8 squaremeters can be a tricky thing. Especially, when you have too much of everything in your old household. You had to be prepared for big dinner parties, right? And it gets even trickier, if you are emotionally attached to things (like I am). But let's face it: What basic equipment has to be in the van? 

On the topic of "too much stuff", also see our post: How to prepare for vanlife

Kitchen area

Well, you probably won't host Christmas dinner parties for your family. Even if you like to invite friends and fellow campers, they often can bring their own cuttlery since they travel in a camper as well. For our two person van-household we pretty much only need: 

  • 2 plates 
  • 2 big bowls (great for Müsli, salads, soups, dessert, ..)
  • 2 enamel cups (that also serve as wine cups)
  • 2 bread knives
  • 2 forkes 
  • 4 small spoons (extra spoons for dips etc)
  • 2 big spoons 
  • ...

You get the picture. After usage, we wash our dishes directly. There is a) no dish washing machine to store dirty cuttlery and b) no space to leave it lying around all day, especially when we move on to another campsite. This means, we really only need what we have to use for one meal. After washing it, it is ready for the next round. So ask yourself: Why would you need 4 forkes? We also made sure to have the one perfect pan for every dish. One big pan with a high border, one strainer, and a medium sized pot for pasta and rice is all we need.

What I thought I would never use and took it with us out of recommendation is a steam cooker top or attachment to our pot. Let me tell you: best decision ever. We use it frequently to steam our veggies while the rice boils under them in the pot. Easy and quick as well. 

The other storage space in the kitchen is taken by plastic or glass storage boxes, one italian espresso maker, milk foaming machine (ok this you don't really need, but we did not want to miss a foamy oat or almond latte), and lots and lots of food. 

Clothes and gear

Honestly: We packed way too much clothing. I have read before that you should take as much clothing as you would need for a 3 weeks vacation. However, standing infront of your closet you cannot do it. I could not do it. So I stuffed every corner of my new tiny closet in the van with clothes. I really thought, or maybe only wanted to believe, that I would need all the shirts and shorts and sport gear. Let me tell you: I do not. I should have listen to the blog posts that told me to only pack for a 3 week vacation. For us, it should have been for a three weeks summer vacation (since we try to avoid winter) plus some comfy warm sweaters and a scarf in addition. We still have to face chilly nights and cold days. That is why you might need a little bit more clothing when you travel through different seasons. 

But really: You wear your favorite clothes and then you wash them and you wear them again. There are so many pieces I haven't touched yet and probably never will. So pack lightly. You can use the extra storage for climbing or surfing equipment - or unique pieces you come across while travelling.  

Activities and leisure

This one is fairly easy: Take whatever you like to enjoy your time. Don't cut short on your euipment you need for sport etc. That is what vanlife is about: More joy in life. Now you have the time for game nights, hobbies and things you have always wanted to try. Only compromising factor: storage space, duhh.

Of course we haved packed things we did not use in the end: tennis rackets for example. We haven't played any tennis since we left Germany - but that is ok. There are so many new and interesting things to do and see. Next time we are in Germany, the tennis rackets will stay with friends, and I have more space for new equipment like the Ukulele I bought ;). 

Toiletry

If you have special skin conditions and need some extra lotions, take them. But otherwise: Pare-down a lot! Really, you, your skin and hair, and the environment will thank you. Take bio-degradable organic basics and you are all set. Maybe you can do with just one soap for every use case? Dr Bronner 18-In-1 natural soap? That would be awesome. Maybe we will reach that one-soap-for-all state at some point in time. But until then, we still have and share

  • a hair shampoo
  • a soap for body/hands/clothes - 18-in-one or something similar
  • mineral sunscreen
  • a tooth paste 
  • body lotion 
  • mosquito repellent
  • lip balms
  • self-made deodorant cream

When your vanlife begins and you still have lotion leftovers, take them with you and use them until they are done. But let me recomend to not buy in bulk beforehand. Other countries have interesting brands and products you never heard of before. You will want to try them. 

Do it yourelf

Besides, when it comes to toiletry, you can actually make many things like deodorant, tooth paste or cleaning supplies by yourself. Use what nature has to offer. This way, you will save money, produce less waste and protect the environment. For this purpose, we always have sodium bicarbonate, vinegar and citric acid on board.
