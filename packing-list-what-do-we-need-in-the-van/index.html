Packing list: What do we need in the van?

Judith

Storage on 8 squaremeters can be a tricky thing. Especially, when you have too much of everything in your household. You had to be prepared for big dinner parties, right? And it gets even trickier, if you are emotionally attached to things (like I am). But let's face it: What has to be in the van? 

Kitchen area

Well, you probably won't host Christmas dinner parties for your family. Even if you like to invite friends and fellow campers, they often can bring their own cuttlery since they travel in a camper as well. For our two person van-household we pretty much only need: 

  • 2 plates 
  • 2 big bowls (great for Müsli, salads, soups, dessert, ..)
  • 2 emaille cups (that also serve as wine cups)
  • 2 bread knives
  • 2 forkes 
  • 4 small spoons (extra spoons for dips etc)
  • 2 big spoons 
  • ...

You get the picture. After usage, we wash our dishes directly. There is a) no dish washing machine to store dirty cuttlery and b) no space to leave it lying around all day, especially when we move on to another campsite. This means, we really only need what we have to use for one meal. After washing it, it is ready for the next round. So ask yourself: Why would you need 4 forkes? Or 4 plates? We also made sure to have the one perfect pan for every dish. One big pan with a high border plus a medium sized pot for pasta and rice is all we need. 

The other storage space in the kitchen is taken by plastic or glass storage boxes, italian espresso maker, milk foaming machine (ok this you don't really need, but we did not want to miss a foamy latte), and lots and lots of food. 

Clothes and gear

Honestly: We packed way too much clothing. I have read before that you should take as much clothing as you would need for a 3 weeks vacation. However, standing infront of your closet you cannot do it. I could not do it. So I stuffed every corner of my new tiny closet in the van with clothes. I really thought, or maybe only wanted to believe, that I would need all the shirts and shorts and sport gear. Let me tell you: You do not. I should have listen to the blog posts that told me to only pack for a 3 week vacation. Of course, you might need a little bit more since you travel through seasons. Even though we try to avoid winter, we still have to face chilly nights and cold days. Some comfy sweaters are a must.  

But really: You wear your favorite clothes and then you wash them and you wear them again. There are so many pieces I haven't touched.
